On the morning of Saturday, October 6, 1973, Muki Betser, the commander of Sayeret Matkal's training squad, was sitting in the front yard of his house in Moshav Nahalal. Like most of Sayeret Matkal's officers, Betser had left the unit a day earlier, intending to spend the weekend, which coincided with Yom Kippur, at home. As an officer in Aman (Military Intelligence Directorate), Betser was aware that both Egyptian and Syrian armies were conducting maneuvers near their borders with Israel.

At 2:05 PM on October 6, Egyptian fighter aircraft attacked Israeli targets in Sinai. Concurrently, Syrian tanks and infantry stormed through Israeli defenses along the armistice lines in the Golan Heights.

Without clear assignments or timely intelligence, Giora Zorea, Sayeret Matkal's commander, and his officers Amiram Levine, Yonatan Netanyahu, and Betser decided to split their forces. Zorea and Levine led one group to Khushniya in the southern Golan, while Netanyahu and Betser took the rest eastward to the IDF 36th Division headquarters in Naffakh. When they arrived on the evening of October 8, the armor had already repelled several Syrian attempts to capture the camp. Brigadier General Rafael Eitan (Raful), the 36th Division commander, welcomed Betser and Netanyahu.

Avraham Arnan knew the unit's whereabouts and anticipated Betser's moves. Arnan had contacted Colonel Zvi Barazani, pressuring him not to deploy Sayeret Matkal on any missions. Concerned that Barazani might yield to Betser's appeals, the cancer-stricken Arnan drove to Naffakh in his car to ensure Barazani held firm. Predictably, Arnan and Betser engaged in a heated exchange, with neither changing position.
